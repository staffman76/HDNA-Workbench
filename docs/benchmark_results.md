# HDNA Benchmark Results

*InspectableTransformer performance vs. vanilla dense transformer, April 2026.*

## Headline

On a 57M-parameter language model trained from scratch on TinyStories (Eldan & Li, 2023) for 5,000 steps on an NVIDIA A100 80GB:

> The HDNA **InspectableTransformer** achieves **99.89%** of vanilla-transformer quality (final val perplexity **1.8615 vs 1.8595**) at **98.78%** of vanilla throughput (**41,432 vs 41,942 tokens/sec**), while exposing the internal trace surface that makes decisions inspectable — per-neuron activation history, expert routing logs, per-head attention statistics.

That's the full trade-off: **within 0.1% on quality, within 1.2% on throughput, +24% peak GPU memory**. For that cost, every forward pass produces an auditable trace of which experts fired, which attention heads specialized on which positions, and how each neuron's activation evolved — surfaced as first-class data structures, not post-hoc instrumentation.

## Key Numbers

### TinyStories 57M, matched A/B (A100 80GB SXM, 5,000 steps, seed=0)

| Metric | Vanilla | Inspectable | Ratio |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|
| Params (total) | 57,491,200 | 57,534,336 | +0.075% |
| Active params per token | 57,491,200 | **38,635,392 (67%)** | — |
| Final val perplexity | 1.8595 | 1.8615 | 1.0011× |
| Throughput (tok/s) | 41,942 | 41,432 | 0.988× |
| Peak GPU memory | 14.8 GB | 18.3 GB | 1.238× |
| Wall time | 65.4 min | 66.1 min | 1.012× |

### Scaling across d_model (A100 80GB, tiny Shakespeare, 1,500 steps)

Single-seed sweep across d_model ∈ {384, 512, 768, 1024}, three conditions per size:

| d_model | Params | Vanilla PPL | Inspectable PPL | PPL Δ | fwd ratio | fwd+bwd ratio |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---:|:---:|
| 384 | 7.3M | 5.35 | 5.64 | +5.4% | 0.87× | 1.21× |
| 512 | 12.8M | 4.94 | 5.03 | +1.8% | 1.03× | 1.11× |
| 768 | 28.7M | 4.63 | **4.61** | **−0.4%** | 1.03× | 1.045× |
| 1024 | 50.8M | 4.52 | 4.56 | +0.9% | 1.02× | 1.045× |

**Interpretation**: the PPL gap closes with scale and flips briefly at d=768. Forward-pass speed at vanilla parity from d=512 onward. Training-cost ratio drops from 1.21× at d=384 to 1.045× at d=1024 — inspectable's overhead effectively disappears as GPU compute dominates kernel-launch overhead.

## Methodology

### Fairness controls

- **Identical configuration** across vanilla and inspectable: same d_model, layers, heads, batch size, sequence length, optimizer, learning rate, training steps, seed.
- **Identical data**: same byte stream, same random-crop sampler driven from a seeded PyTorch `Generator`.
- **Identical evaluation**: same 20-batch validation subset, same perplexity calculation (`exp(cross_entropy_loss)`).
- **Both conditions run back-to-back on the same physical GPU**, no cross-contamination.

### What's different

- **Architecture only**: vanilla uses dense feed-forward; inspectable uses a top-2 routed MoE with 4 experts per layer plus tagged multi-head attention and per-head gating.
- **Trace recording disabled** for both (`return_trace=False`) — we're measuring the production inference path, not the debugging path. Trace mode is separately characterized in `experiments/parity_transformer/README.md`.

### Why byte-level tokenization

Byte-level (vocab=256, raw UTF-8) removes every external tokenizer dependency. Every number in this document is reproducible from a `pip install` + corpus download. Trade-off: absolute PPL numbers aren't directly comparable to published BPE / tiktoken TinyStories results. The A/B ratio — which is what matters for the architecture claim — is unaffected.

- **TinyStories** (Eldan & Li, 2023), mirrored at `https://huggingface.co/datasets/roneneldan/TinyStories`. 199M tokens training / 1M tokens val (byte-level).
- **tiny Shakespeare** (Karpathy), 1MB, cached automatically on first run.

## What this does and doesn't prove

### What it proves

- The HDNA inspectable architecture is **cost-competitive** with vanilla transformers at real-LM scale on a standard public corpus.
- The inspectability surface (routed experts, tagged attention, per-neuron memory) is **not paid for in quality loss** — matched within noise.
- The implementation runs on **commodity single-GPU hardware** without heroic engineering.

### What it doesn't prove

- **Inspectability value**: this benchmark confirms the *cost* of inspectability is low; it does not yet demonstrate a concrete debugging or audit win that vanilla transformers can't match. A case study showing HDNA's trace surfacing a specific model pathology faster than existing tools (TransformerLens, nnsight) is the next artifact needed.
- **Scaling beyond 57M params**: tested up to 51M params on tiny Shakespeare and 57M on TinyStories. Larger-model training runs (200M-1B params) remain untested; based on the narrowing-overhead trend they should hold, but that's inference, not measurement.
- **Multi-seed variance**: single seed per condition. PPL differences below ~2% are within the expected single-seed noise band; multi-seed averaging (~$18 additional cloud cost) would tighten the claim.
- **Mixed-precision production numbers**: these numbers are FP32. BF16 + `torch.compile` would give a more accurate picture of production deployment; not measured here.
